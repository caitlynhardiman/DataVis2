Tourism in Australia: 2009-2019

Exploring tourism trends for international visitors and Australian residents over the past decade

Where do tourists like to go in Australia?

The choropleth maps below show which regions in Australia are visited the most by each of the three different demographics. Unfortunately in the international case, data was only available on a state basis. Note also that areas in white are those with missing data. We can see from these maps that the capital cities attract the most tourists (as would be expected), and that the east coast of Australia is favoured as a destination in all cases.

Why do tourists travel in Australia?

People travel in Australia for three main reasons: for a holiday, to visit family and friends and to work. There are also other reasons, such as education and searching for employment opportunities. We can compare the main reasons for travel in Australia of international visitors and residents by looking at the percentage of the total amount of visitors by purpose for each. Hover over the bars for more information, and select different years on the filter to see how these changed with time.

How long do tourists stay?

We might expect that the more visitors to Australia, the longer they would stay, but this is actually not the case. We can see on the bubble plot to the right that there is in fact a negative relationship between these two variables: countries with less visitors tend to stay longer in Australia. One reason this may be is due to the proximity of the country: looking at New Zealand, our closest neighbour, we see that New Zealanders visit often but for short times. They also spend much less money than most of the other countries. We can therefore conclude that the further a tourist travels to Australia, the more likely they are to stay longer and spend more money.

Use the filter beneath the plot to select a year to look at, and hover over each dot for individual information. If you wish to look at information about visitors from a specific continent, select the continent name in the legend to filter out the other data.
